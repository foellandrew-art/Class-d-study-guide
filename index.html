⚠️ The Risk Profile

Novice drivers face significantly higher crash risks during their first year of licensure. Inexperience with hazard anticipation, combined with distractions (passengers, phones), leads to critical errors in judgment and vehicle control.

🛡️ The Solution: GDL & Supervision

Minnesota's Graduated Driver Licensing (GDL) program mandates 50 hours of supervised practice (15 night) and restricts high-risk scenarios (late-night driving, peer passengers) during the Provisional phase.

“Even after your teen is licensed, keep riding along. Reinforce scanning, space cushions, and wise decisions under pressure.”

The Graduated Licensing System

Minnesota law uses a phased approach to earning full driving privileges. This section breaks down the requirements for the Instruction Permit, Provisional License, and full Class D License. Select a phase to explore rules, restrictions, and parent responsibilities.

⚖️ Vanessa's Law (Minn. Stat. § 171.05)

For unlicensed teens, a crash-related moving violation or alcohol/controlled substance violation can block licensure until 18. At 18, they must pass knowledge testing, meet reinstatement requirements, and hold a permit for six months.

⏱️ The Basic Speed Law

Statutory limits (10 mph alleys, 30 mph urban, 55 mph rural) apply when signs are absent. But the Basic Speed Law requires that you never drive faster than is reasonable under existing conditions (traffic, weather, visibility, surface).

🔀 The Zipper Merge

When a lane ends in congestion, stay in your lane up to the merge point, then alternate one‑for‑one. Early merging creates turbulence and longer backups.

Scenario: “Right Lane Ends 1 Mile.”
Action: Stay in the right lane, drive to the merge point, and take turns.

🛑 Uncontrolled Intersections

When two vehicles arrive simultaneously at an uncontrolled intersection, the driver on the left yields to the driver on the right. Scan early, communicate intentions, and proceed cautiously.

Hazards, Sharing the Road & Impairment

Protect vulnerable road users and recognize how impairment degrades performance long before obvious signs appear. Review truck No‑Zones, school bus law, hydroplaning, and BAC effects.

Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC) Effects

Judgment and hazard detection degrade quickly as BAC rises—well before 0.08.

DWI & “Not a Drop”

Legal limit for 21+ is 0.08. For under 21, “Not a Drop” means any detectable alcohol results in penalties. Implied Consent: refusal to test triggers license consequences.

🚛 Trucks & No‑Zones

Large trucks have deep blind spots (front, rear, and both sides). If you can’t see the driver’s face in the mirror, they can’t see you. Give wide berth; never pass a turning truck on the right.
